The allotment system that we have today dates back to the nineteenth century

In 1908 the Small Holdings and Allotments Act was introduced meaning that local authorities had to provide allotments

The Dig for Victory campaign during the 1940's encouraged people to use allotments. There were over 1.5 million across the UK

Allotments were made available to everyone at the end of the First World War as a way of assisting returning service men

The 1970's saw a spike in demand most likely fueled by trade union unrest and depicted in the BBC television show The Good Life
